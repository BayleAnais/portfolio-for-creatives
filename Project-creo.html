  • Creo Toys

    Branding / Packaging / Advertising

    • The way society genders toys causes developmental disparities between boys and girls and perpetrates social stereotypes. These imbalances can persit into adulthood. Creo tackles this issue early by building equality through toys that enable balanced learning opportunities.

      • Key Insights

      • People know toys are gendered. But many don’t know how different the skillsets taught by traditionally masculine or feminine toys are.
      • Toys teach kids lifelong skills. They affect a person’s self image and interpersonal relationships from a young age.
      • Children start to catch onto what they consider ‘appropriate’ toys as early as 2-3.
      • Some children are innately drawn gender-conforming toys. Often, this is compounded by socialization. Regardless of where they may fall, every child benefits from a well-balanced toybox.

      • The Products

      • Creo takes action to combat the issues arising from gender-typed play through toys that blend physical and educational attributes of various toy types. These give children a chance do learn a wide range of skills.

      • Advertising

      • This animated ad makes a big topic easily digestible. Using a nutrition metaphor to highlight the importance of a well-balanced toybox, this advertisement establishes Creo as a solution to the problems caused by gendering toys.
